# 📈 Project 2: Normal Distribution of Student Exam Scores

## 📌 Overview

This project explains how student exam scores follow a **Normal Distribution (Bell Curve)**. It demonstrates statistical concepts such as mean, standard deviation, and the empirical rule for performance analysis.

## 📚 Domain Explanation

Student exam scores typically form a symmetric bell-shaped curve:

* Most students score near the average
* Few students score extremely high or low

## 📊 Assumptions

* Mean (μ) = 70 marks
* Standard Deviation (σ) = 10 marks

## 📉 Graph Explanation

* Bell-shaped normal curve
* Center at mean (70)
* X-axis → Marks
* Y-axis → Frequency

## 📐 Empirical Rule (68–95–99.7 Rule)

### 🔹 μ ± 1σ (60–80 marks)

* Covers 68% of students
* Majority fall in this range

### 🔹 μ ± 2σ (50–90 marks)

* Covers 95% of students
* Almost all students included

### 🔹 μ ± 3σ (40–100 marks)

* Covers 99.7% of students
* Very few extreme values

## 📊 Representation Methods

* Histogram visualization
* Normal curve plotting
* Skewed distribution comparison
* Mean vs Median vs Mode analysis

## 🎯 Importance

* Performance analysis
* Academic grading systems
* Identifying toppers and weak students
* Educational decision-making
